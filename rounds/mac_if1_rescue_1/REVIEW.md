# MAC-IF1 supervisor review

## Decision

`MAC-IF1` is admitted as a conditional rescue lead because it replaces generic composition matching with a falsifiable spatial rule and preserves a low-cost solid-particle route. It does not pass any experiment-release level.

## Three owner gates

| Gate | Current result | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Complete biomimetic story | revise | Real mature-biofilm class enrichment and macrolide lipid-interface topology are both supported, but their causal connection is still a hypothesis and unrelated cationic amphiphiles are strong counterexamples. |
| Material and experimental science | revise | The artificial spatial rule, candidate route and matched controls are defined, but shallow pore-wall placement has not been manufactured or measured. |
| Engineering validation | revise | Millimeter particles and common reagents are plausible, but UV/solvent mass balance, wet strength, secondary-effluent columns, regeneration and cost remain untested. |

Numerical scoring is not applied because all three non-compensable gates have not passed.

## Accepted changes relative to MAC-BI1

- Uniform hydrolyzed MAn–St–DVB is no longer presented as the main biomimetic material.
- Mature wastewater biofilm is the real environmental observation; phospholipid work supplies a bounded spatial mechanism rather than a complete treatment analogy.
- The material claim is limited to shallow spatial adjacency, not carboxylate–hydrophobic synergy in general.
- Old MAC-BI1, commercial mixed-mode resin and GAC remain visible controls.
- The route generates a distinctive failure prediction: increasing acid coverage can reduce performance if it masks the adjacent low-polarity wall.

## Conditions for the next review

1. Freeze B0 with exact lipid compositions, drug panel, concentrations, analytical recovery, replication and class-boundary stop rule.
2. Freeze M0 with a same-batch bead source, photograft matrix, spatial measurements, accessible-acid matching and pore-blocking stop rule.
3. Do not release C0 until both B0 and M0 pass.
4. If B0 fails, remove class-level biomimetic language. If M0 fails, remove directional/shallow language. If either label is removed, reopen whether a simpler engineering resin is worth pursuing.
